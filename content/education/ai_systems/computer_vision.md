# Computer Vision

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Computer vision enables AI to interpret visual market data such as charts, order-book heatmaps, and depth ladders.

### Origin
Advanced rapidly after convolutional neural networks in 2012; applied to finance with the rise of high-resolution market data visualization.

### Why It Matters
Many market signals are visual patterns that traditional numerical models miss.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Models process candlestick charts, volume profiles, and order-flow visualizations to detect patterns and anomalies.

### How It Behaves
Excels at spotting recurring formations but can be fooled by novel regime shifts.

### Key Data to Watch
- Pattern recognition confidence
- False-positive rates on historical regimes

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Proprietary trading firms use vision models for tape reading and microstructure analysis.

### Professional Use Cases
- Automated chart pattern detection
- Order-book visualization anomaly alerts
- Cross-asset visual correlation mapping

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Technical Agent**: Processes live chart and heatmap imagery.
- **Liquidity Agent**: Interprets depth ladder visuals for hidden liquidity.

### Key Takeaways
Computer vision turns visual market intuition into scalable, repeatable intelligence.
