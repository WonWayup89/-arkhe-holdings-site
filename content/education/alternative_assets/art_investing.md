# Art Investing

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Art investing involves purchasing fine art and contemporary works with the expectation of capital appreciation.

### Origin
Institutional art investment expanded in the 1980s with freeport storage.

### Why It Matters
Art offers low correlation to financial markets and serves as a store of value.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Valuation depends on artist reputation, provenance, and auction comparables.

### How It Behaves
Prices are driven by wealth concentration and cultural narratives.

### Key Data to Watch
- Mei Moses Art Index
- Auction turnover and unsold rates

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Family offices use art as a balance-sheet asset and collateral.

### Professional Use Cases
- Art-secured financing
- Diversified art funds

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Portfolio Agent**: Incorporates art as a low-correlation alternative.

### Key Takeaways
Art investing requires specialized expertise and long-term horizons.
