# Collectibles

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Collectibles are tangible assets such as rare wines, watches, art, and classic cars purchased for both aesthetic and investment value.

### Origin
Institutional interest grew in the 2000s with fractional ownership platforms.

### Why It Matters
Collectibles provide diversification with low correlation to traditional financial assets.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Valuation relies on scarcity, provenance, and demand. Liquidity is limited and transaction costs are high.

### How It Behaves
Prices are driven by cultural trends; returns are lumpy.

### Key Data to Watch
- Auction realization rates
- Luxury investment indices
- Insurance and storage costs

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Family offices allocate small percentages via specialized managers.

### Professional Use Cases
- Diversified luxury asset baskets
- Art-secured lending

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Portfolio Agent**: Models correlation and inflation-hedging properties.

### Key Takeaways
Collectibles are a niche but effective diversifier when professionally managed.
