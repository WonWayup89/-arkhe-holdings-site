# Cash Equivalents

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Cash equivalents are highly liquid, short-term instruments that preserve capital.

### Origin
Treasury bills became the institutional standard after the 1970s.

### Why It Matters
Cash equivalents serve as dry powder and the ultimate risk-off asset.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Instruments include T-bills and money-market funds tied to policy rates.

### How It Behaves
Real yields turn negative during easing.

### Key Data to Watch
- Short-term Treasury yields
- Reverse repo usage

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Institutions manage cash equivalents for liquidity and policy implementation.

### Professional Use Cases
- Liquidity buffers
- Collateral in repo markets

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Liquidity Agent**: Monitors cash flows as risk appetite indicators.

### Key Takeaways
Cash equivalents are the tactical reserve of institutional portfolios.
