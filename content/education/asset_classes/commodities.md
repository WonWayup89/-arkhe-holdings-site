# Commodities

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Commodities are physical goods traded on futures exchanges that serve as economic inputs.

### Origin
Organized futures trading began in the 19th century.

### Why It Matters
Commodities act as inflation hedges and reflect real economic dynamics.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Futures contracts enable price discovery and hedging.

### How It Behaves
Commodities are pro-cyclical and exhibit mean reversion.

### Key Data to Watch
- Inventory levels
- Futures curve shape

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
CTAs and producers use futures for tactical overlays.

### Professional Use Cases
- Inflation-protected portfolios
- Basis trading

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Macro Agent**: Correlates prices with growth and inflation.

### Key Takeaways
Commodities are the real-economy counterpart to financial assets.
