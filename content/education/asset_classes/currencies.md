# Currencies

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Currencies are fiat money issued by governments that serve as the medium of exchange.

### Origin
The modern floating exchange-rate system began in 1971.

### Why It Matters
Currency movements drive global capital flows and investment returns.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Currencies trade 24 hours with prices driven by interest-rate differentials.

### How It Behaves
Major reserve currencies exhibit mean-reverting behavior.

### Key Data to Watch
- Real effective exchange rates
- Interest-rate differentials

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Global macro funds manage large currency exposures.

### Professional Use Cases
- Carry-trade portfolios
- Currency hedging

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Macro Agent**: Tracks rate differentials and flows.

### Key Takeaways
Currencies are the transmission mechanism of global monetary policy.
