# Derivatives

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Derivatives are financial contracts whose value is derived from an underlying asset.

### Origin
Modern derivatives expanded after the 1970s.

### Why It Matters
Derivatives enable risk transfer and efficient capital allocation.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Common types include futures, options, and swaps priced under no-arbitrage assumptions.

### How It Behaves
Derivatives amplify gains and losses and create feedback loops.

### Key Data to Watch
- Open interest
- Implied volatility

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Institutions use derivatives for hedging and relative-value trading.

### Professional Use Cases
- Portfolio insurance
- Volatility arbitrage

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Execution Agent**: Optimizes derivative hedging.

### Key Takeaways
Derivatives are the precision tools of professional risk management.
