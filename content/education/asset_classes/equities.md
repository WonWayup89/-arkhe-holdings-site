# Equities

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Equities represent ownership shares in companies and entitle holders to residual cash flows.

### Origin
Modern equity markets originated with the Dutch East India Company in 1602.

### Why It Matters
Equities are the primary vehicle for long-term capital appreciation.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Equities trade on exchanges with price discovery driven by supply and demand.

### How It Behaves
Equities exhibit long-term upward drift with periodic drawdowns tied to earnings cycles.

### Key Data to Watch
- Earnings growth
- Institutional ownership
- Buyback and dividend yields

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Institutions dominate volume through algorithmic execution and dark pools.

### Professional Use Cases
- Factor-based equity portfolios
- Sector rotation strategies

### Key Takeaways
Equities are the foundational growth asset class.
