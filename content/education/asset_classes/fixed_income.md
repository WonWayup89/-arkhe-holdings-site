# Fixed Income

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Fixed income securities are debt instruments that provide periodic interest payments.

### Origin
Government bonds became the global benchmark after World War II.

### Why It Matters
Fixed income serves as the risk-free benchmark and portfolio stabilizer.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Bond prices move inversely to yields. Duration measures interest-rate sensitivity.

### How It Behaves
Fixed income exhibits negative correlation to equities in flight-to-quality regimes.

### Key Data to Watch
- Yield curve shape
- Credit spreads

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Institutions manage fixed-income portfolios for liability matching.

### Professional Use Cases
- Duration hedging
- Credit versus rates relative value

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Macro Agent**: Interprets yield curve signals.
- **Risk Agent**: Models duration risk.

### Key Takeaways
Fixed income is the foundational pricing mechanism for all risk assets.
