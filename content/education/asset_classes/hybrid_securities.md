# Hybrid Securities

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Hybrid securities combine debt and equity features such as convertible bonds.

### Origin
Convertibles emerged in the 19th century.

### Why It Matters
Hybrids bridge fixed income and equity allocations.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Pricing reflects credit risk and the embedded equity option.

### How It Behaves
Hybrids behave like bonds when out-of-the-money and like equity when in-the-money.

### Key Data to Watch
- Conversion premium
- Embedded option Greeks

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Hedge funds exploit mispricings while corporates issue hybrids for lower-cost financing.

### Professional Use Cases
- Convertible arbitrage
- Mezzanine financing

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Technical Agent**: Monitors delta and gamma exposure.

### Key Takeaways
Hybrid securities provide sophisticated risk-reward engineering.
