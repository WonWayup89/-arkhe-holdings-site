# Bridges

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Bridges are protocols that transfer assets or data between different blockchains.

### Origin
Bridges expanded rapidly after 2021 as multi-chain ecosystems grew and users needed to move assets between Ethereum, Layer 2 networks, and alternative Layer 1s.

### Why It Matters
Bridges enable capital mobility and composability across fragmented blockchain networks.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Bridges use lock-and-mint, burn-and-release, or liquidity pool models. Security relies on validators, economic incentives, smart contracts, or cryptographic proofs.

### How It Behaves
Bridges are frequent targets of exploits because they hold large value and depend on complex trust assumptions. Bridge flows can also reveal capital rotation between ecosystems.

### Key Data to Watch
- Total value bridged
- Bridge TVL and utilization
- Security incident history
- Cross-chain flow volume
- Validator or relayer structure

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Institutions use audited, permissioned, or highly reputable bridges for cross-chain treasury and tokenized asset movement. Risk controls often restrict bridge exposure.

### Professional Use Cases
- Multi-chain portfolio management
- Cross-chain collateral rebalancing
- Arbitrage between ecosystems
- Tokenized asset distribution

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Liquidity Agent**: Tracks bridge flows and arbitrage opportunities.
- **Risk Agent**: Scores bridge security and counterparty assumptions.
- **Portfolio Agent**: Adjusts chain exposure based on flow migration.

### Key Takeaways
Bridges are the connective infrastructure of multi-chain finance and a critical point of systemic risk.
