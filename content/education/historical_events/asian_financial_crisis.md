# Asian Financial Crisis

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
The 1997–1998 Asian Financial Crisis was a severe currency and banking crisis across East Asia.

### Origin
Triggered by current-account deficits and fixed exchange rates.

### Why It Matters
The crisis demonstrated risks of currency pegs and hot money flows.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Speculative attacks forced devaluations and IMF bailouts.

### How It Behaves
Capital flight created classic emerging-market crisis dynamics.

### Key Data to Watch
- Foreign exchange reserve depletion

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Global macro funds profited from shorting Asian currencies.

### Professional Use Cases
- Emerging-market crisis modeling

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Macro Agent**: Uses the crisis as a currency peg vulnerability template.

### Key Takeaways
The Asian crisis is the textbook case of fixed exchange-rate failures.
