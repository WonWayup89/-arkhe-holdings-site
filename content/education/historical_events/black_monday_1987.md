# Black Monday 1987

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Black Monday was the largest one-day percentage decline in U.S. stock market history on October 19, 1987.

### Origin
The crash followed rising rates and mechanical selling from portfolio insurance.

### Why It Matters
Black Monday exposed risks of automated trading strategies.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Portfolio insurance created a feedback loop of selling as prices fell.

### How It Behaves
The crash was a liquidity event rather than fundamental collapse.

### Key Data to Watch
- Portfolio insurance selling volume

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
The event led to circuit breakers and market structure reforms.

### Professional Use Cases
- Study of liquidity black holes

Black Monday remains the canonical example of mechanical feedback loops in markets.
