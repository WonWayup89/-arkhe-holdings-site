# European Debt Crisis

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
The European Debt Crisis (2010–2012) was a sovereign debt and banking crisis in the Eurozone periphery.

### Origin
Triggered by high debt levels, fiscal deficits, and loss of monetary sovereignty.

### Why It Matters
The crisis exposed flaws in the Eurozone architecture and the power of ECB intervention.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Rising sovereign yields, bank-sovereign doom loops, and capital flight created a self-reinforcing crisis.

### How It Behaves
Contagion spread rapidly until ECB bond-buying programs restored confidence.

### Key Data to Watch
- Sovereign yield spreads
- Target2 balances
- Bank-sovereign holdings

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Global macro funds positioned for ECB policy responses and periphery recovery.

### Professional Use Cases
- Sovereign debt relative value
- Eurozone breakup scenario planning

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Macro Agent**: Tracks sovereign risk and ECB transmission.

### Key Takeaways
The European debt crisis demonstrated the fragility of monetary unions without fiscal union.
