# Housing Bubble

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
The mid-2000s U.S. housing bubble was a rapid increase in home prices driven by loose lending.

### Origin
The bubble built from 2000 to 2006 and burst in 2007–2008.

### Why It Matters
The bubble and crash triggered the global financial crisis.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Subprime lending and securitization created a price-credit feedback loop.

### How It Behaves
Housing bubbles are slow to build and extremely painful to unwind.

### Key Data to Watch
- Price-to-income ratios
- Mortgage debt service ratios

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Banks learned the importance of mortgage portfolio stress testing.

### Professional Use Cases
- Housing cycle analysis

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Macro Agent**: Monitors housing leverage as recession precursor.

### Key Takeaways
The housing bubble showed how credit-fueled asset bubbles threaten global stability.
