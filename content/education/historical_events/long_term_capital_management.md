# Long Term Capital Management

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
LTCM was a highly leveraged hedge fund that collapsed in 1998 after the Russian debt default.

### Origin
Founded in 1994 by Nobel laureates using convergence arbitrage.

### Why It Matters
LTCM demonstrated systemic risk from crowded, leveraged trades.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Convergence trades became crowded; Russia default triggered liquidity evaporation.

### How It Behaves
Model-driven strategies failed when liquidity and correlation assumptions broke.

### Key Data to Watch
- Leverage ratios
- Position concentration

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
The crisis led to stricter risk management practices.

### Professional Use Cases
- Crowded trade risk study

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Risk Agent**: Uses LTCM as a template for liquidity shocks.

### Key Takeaways
LTCM is the canonical example of model failure under regime change.
