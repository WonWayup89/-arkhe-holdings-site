# Capital Allocation

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Capital allocation is the process of distributing resources across investments to maximize long-term value.

### Origin
Modern frameworks were formalized in the 1950s–1960s.

### Why It Matters
Effective capital allocation separates superior compounders.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Institutions use hurdle rates and risk-adjusted metrics to rank opportunities.

### How It Behaves
Allocation decisions are path-dependent and regime-sensitive.

### Key Data to Watch
- Return on invested capital
- Capital deployment pace

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Hedge funds run continuous optimization models.

### Professional Use Cases
- Dynamic risk-budget allocation
- Cross-strategy rebalancing

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Portfolio Agent**: Performs autonomous capital allocation.

### Key Takeaways
Capital allocation is the ultimate expression of investment philosophy.
