# Family Offices

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Family offices are private wealth management entities serving ultra-high-net-worth families.

### Origin
The modern model traces to the Rockefeller family.

### Why It Matters
Family offices are flexible, long-term capital allocators.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Single-family and multi-family offices pursue direct deals and alternatives.

### How It Behaves
Investment mandates range from conservative to highly opportunistic.

### Key Data to Watch
- Allocation to alternatives
- Governance structures

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Family offices maintain internal teams for venture and private equity.

### Professional Use Cases
- Direct private investments
- Co-investments

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Portfolio Agent**: Models long-horizon, tax-aware allocation.

### Key Takeaways
Family offices combine institutional rigor with entrepreneurial flexibility.
