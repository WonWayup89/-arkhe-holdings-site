# Pension Funds

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Pension funds manage retirement savings for employees of corporations and governments.

### Origin
Modern pension funds grew after World War II.

### Why It Matters
Pension funds are among the largest long-term capital allocators.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Funds match long-duration liabilities with assets using liability-driven investing.

### How It Behaves
Funding ratios drive investment policy.

### Key Data to Watch
- Funded status
- Allocation to alternatives

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Pension funds allocate to private markets and use sophisticated asset-liability modeling.

### Professional Use Cases
- Liability-driven overlays
- Private market co-investments

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Portfolio Agent**: Optimizes under long-term liability constraints.

### Key Takeaways
Pension funds shape capital markets through long-term liability matching.
