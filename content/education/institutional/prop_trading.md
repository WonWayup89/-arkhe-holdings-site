# Prop Trading

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Proprietary trading is trading an institution’s own capital for direct profit.

### Origin
Prop trading was a major profit center until the Volcker Rule.

### Why It Matters
Prop trading drives market liquidity and price discovery.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Prop desks use leverage and quantitative models across asset classes.

### How It Behaves
Performance is variable and can create systemic risk when leveraged positions unwind.

### Key Data to Watch
- Value-at-risk limits
- Return on equity

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Prop trading has migrated to hedge funds and specialized firms post-Volcker.

### Professional Use Cases
- Statistical arbitrage
- Macro directional trading

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Risk Agent**: Monitors firm-wide prop exposure.

### Key Takeaways
Proprietary trading remains a core driver of market liquidity.
