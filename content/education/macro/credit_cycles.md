# Credit Cycles

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Credit cycles are the expansion and contraction of credit availability and leverage.

### Origin
Credit cycle theory was developed by economists studying business cycles.

### Why It Matters
Credit cycles drive economic booms and busts.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Expansion phases feature rising leverage; contraction phases feature deleveraging.

### How It Behaves
Credit cycles are longer and more powerful than business cycles.

### Key Data to Watch
- Total debt-to-GDP
- Credit impulse

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Macro investors position for different phases of the credit cycle.

### Professional Use Cases
- Credit cycle regime overlays

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Macro Agent**: Maps position within credit cycles.

### Key Takeaways
Credit cycles are the primary driver of major economic regime shifts.
