# Dollar System

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
The dollar system is the global network of dollar-denominated trade, reserves, and financial flows.

### Origin
The dollar became dominant after Bretton Woods in 1944.

### Why It Matters
Dollar system mechanics determine global liquidity and U.S. economic privilege.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
The dollar’s reserve status creates structural demand through trade invoicing and reserve holdings.

### How It Behaves
The system is resilient but sensitive to U.S. policy and geopolitical shifts.

### Key Data to Watch
- Dollar share in global reserves
- SWIFT transaction shares

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Central banks manage dollar reserves with liquidity and safety constraints.

### Professional Use Cases
- Dollar strength forecasting
- De-dollarization monitoring

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Macro Agent**: Tracks dollar system dynamics.

### Key Takeaways
The dollar system is the backbone of global finance and U.S. economic influence.
