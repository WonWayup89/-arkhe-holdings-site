# Global Liquidity

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Global liquidity is the total availability of money and credit worldwide.

### Origin
The concept gained prominence after 2008 when central bank balance sheets became the dominant source.

### Why It Matters
Global liquidity drives risk asset prices and volatility.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Liquidity is supplied by central banks, commercial banks, and shadow banking.

### How It Behaves
Rising liquidity supports risk-on environments.

### Key Data to Watch
- Global central bank balance sheets

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Institutions increase risk during liquidity expansion.

### Professional Use Cases
- Liquidity-driven asset allocation

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Macro Agent**: Synthesizes liquidity indicators.

### Key Takeaways
Global liquidity is the master variable of market regimes.
