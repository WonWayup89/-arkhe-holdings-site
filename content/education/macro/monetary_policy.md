# Monetary Policy

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Monetary policy is the management of money supply and interest rates by central banks.

### Origin
Modern central banking began with the Federal Reserve in 1913.

### Why It Matters
Monetary policy is the primary tool for managing inflation and growth.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Tools include policy rates, forward guidance, and quantitative easing.

### How It Behaves
Policy is most effective when inflation expectations are anchored.

### Key Data to Watch
- Policy rate and dot plot

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Institutions position according to expected policy paths.

### Professional Use Cases
- Rate path forecasting

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Macro Agent**: Models central bank reaction functions.

### Key Takeaways
Monetary policy is the central mechanism of economic management.
