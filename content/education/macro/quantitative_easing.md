# Quantitative Easing

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Quantitative easing is central bank purchase of securities to inject liquidity.

### Origin
First implemented by the Federal Reserve in 2008.

### Why It Matters
QE is the primary non-conventional tool when rates are near zero.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
QE expands the central bank balance sheet and lowers long-term rates.

### How It Behaves
QE compresses risk premia and supports asset prices.

### Key Data to Watch
- Central bank balance sheet size

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Institutions front-run QE announcements.

### Professional Use Cases
- Duration extension trades

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Macro Agent**: Tracks QE liquidity transmission.

### Key Takeaways
Quantitative easing is central bank liquidity injection at scale.
