# Quantitative Tightening

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Quantitative tightening is the reduction of a central bank’s balance sheet.

### Origin
First implemented by the Federal Reserve in 2017.

### Why It Matters
QT removes liquidity and can tighten financial conditions.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Balance-sheet runoff reduces bank reserves.

### How It Behaves
Markets often price QT in advance.

### Key Data to Watch
- Central bank balance-sheet contraction pace

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Institutions reduce risk exposure during QT.

### Professional Use Cases
- Defensive positioning

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Macro Agent**: Monitors QT transmission.

### Key Takeaways
Quantitative tightening is the deliberate withdrawal of central bank liquidity.
