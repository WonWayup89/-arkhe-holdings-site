# Treasury Markets

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Treasury markets are the markets for government debt securities issued by sovereigns.

### Origin
U.S. Treasuries became the global benchmark after World War II.

### Why It Matters
Treasuries are the risk-free benchmark for all financial pricing.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Treasuries serve as collateral, safe-haven assets, and monetary policy transmission tools.

### How It Behaves
Treasury yields reflect growth, inflation, and policy expectations.

### Key Data to Watch
- Yield curve shape
- Term premium

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Central banks and institutions manage massive Treasury portfolios.

### Professional Use Cases
- Duration hedging
- Safe-haven positioning

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Macro Agent**: Interprets Treasury signals as recession indicators.

### Key Takeaways
Treasury markets are the foundation of global financial pricing.
