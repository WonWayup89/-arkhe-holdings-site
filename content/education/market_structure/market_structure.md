**Market Structure**

### Beginner Level

**What Is Market Structure?**  
Market structure is the system through which orders are placed, matched, routed, executed, and reflected in prices.

**Origin**  
Market structure evolved from open-outcry trading floors to electronic exchanges, dark pools, algorithmic routing, and high-frequency trading.

**Why It Matters**  
Understanding market structure helps traders understand liquidity, slippage, spreads, execution quality, and why prices move the way they do.

### Intermediate Level

**Market Mechanics**  
Core concepts include order books, bid-ask spreads, market orders, limit orders, slippage, depth, liquidity, support, resistance, and market regimes.

**How Market Structure Behaves**  
Structure changes during volatility. Liquidity can disappear, spreads can widen, and price can move rapidly through thin order books.

**Key Data to Watch**  
Order-book depth, spread, volume, volatility, liquidity zones, dark-pool prints, and execution slippage.

### Advanced Level

**Institutional Behavior**  
Institutions exploit microstructure using smart order routing, VWAP, TWAP, dark pools, and algorithmic execution.

**Professional Use Cases**  
Execution optimization, order-flow analysis, liquidity mapping, slippage reduction, and trade timing.

**AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe**  
Liquidity Agent maps real-time market depth. Technical Agent monitors order-flow changes. Execution Agent chooses venue and timing.

**Key Takeaways**  
Market structure is the plumbing underneath every trade.
