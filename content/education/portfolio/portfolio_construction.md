**Portfolio Construction**

### Beginner Level

**What Is Portfolio Construction?**  
Portfolio construction is the process of combining assets to achieve specific goals such as growth, income, preservation, or risk-adjusted returns.

**Origin**  
Modern Portfolio Theory, introduced by Harry Markowitz in 1952, formalized the idea that diversification can improve risk-adjusted returns.

**Why It Matters**  
A strong portfolio can survive market cycles and compound wealth more effectively than isolated trades.

### Intermediate Level

**Market Mechanics**  
Portfolio construction includes asset allocation, diversification, rebalancing, correlation management, factor exposure, risk parity, and tax-aware positioning.

**How Portfolio Construction Behaves**  
Diversification works best when correlations remain stable. During crises, correlations often rise and portfolios may need dynamic risk controls.

**Key Data to Watch**  
Correlation matrix, volatility, drawdown, efficient frontier, rebalancing frequency, risk contribution, and factor exposure.

### Advanced Level

**Institutional Behavior**  
Endowments, pensions, and sovereign wealth funds use diversified allocations including public equities, bonds, real estate, private equity, hedge funds, and alternatives.

**Professional Use Cases**  
Factor-based allocation, dynamic macro overlays, rebalancing, risk budgeting, and tax-aware portfolio management.

**Key Takeaways**  
Portfolio construction turns individual ideas into a durable wealth system.
