# Cognitive Bias

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Cognitive biases are systematic errors in thinking that affect investment decisions.

### Origin
Formalized by Kahneman and Tversky in the 1970s.

### Why It Matters
Biases are the persistent source of market inefficiency.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Common biases include overconfidence, loss aversion, and confirmation bias.

### How It Behaves
Biases are amplified by leverage and performance pressure.

### Key Data to Watch
- Disposition effect metrics
- Forecast accuracy overconfidence

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Firms design processes to mitigate biases.

### Professional Use Cases
- Bias-aware investment committees

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Supervisor Agent**: Enforces debiasing rules.

### Key Takeaways
Cognitive biases are the reason systematic approaches generate alpha.
