# Herd Behavior

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Herd behavior is the tendency of investors to follow the majority rather than independent analysis.

### Origin
Documented for centuries and formalized in behavioral finance.

### Why It Matters
Herding amplifies market moves and creates bubbles.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Herding is driven by information cascades and social proof.

### How It Behaves
Herd behavior is strongest at market extremes.

### Key Data to Watch
- Positioning concentration
- Media sentiment uniformity

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Professionals monitor and sometimes fade herd behavior.

### Professional Use Cases
- Contrarian positioning

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Sentiment Agent**: Detects herd formation.

### Key Takeaways
Herd behavior is a powerful driver of market inefficiency.
