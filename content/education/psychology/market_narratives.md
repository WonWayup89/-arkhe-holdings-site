# Market Narratives

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Market narratives are the prevailing stories that investors use to explain and justify price movements.

### Origin
Narrative economics was formalized by Robert Shiller.

### Why It Matters
Narratives drive capital allocation and can become self-fulfilling.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Narratives spread through media and social channels, influencing positioning and valuation.

### How It Behaves
Narratives strengthen during trends and collapse when contradicted by data.

### Key Data to Watch
- Narrative consistency in media
- Sentiment uniformity

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Institutions monitor narrative shifts for tactical positioning.

### Professional Use Cases
- Narrative-driven thematic rotation
- Short-selling of broken narratives

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Sentiment Agent**: Tracks narrative formation and decay.

### Key Takeaways
Market narratives are powerful drivers of capital flows and regime changes.
