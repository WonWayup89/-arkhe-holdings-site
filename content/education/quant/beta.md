# Beta

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Beta measures an asset’s sensitivity to market movements.

### Origin
Beta was formalized in the Capital Asset Pricing Model in the 1960s.

### Why It Matters
Beta is the primary measure of systematic risk in portfolio construction.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Beta is the slope coefficient from regression of asset returns on market returns.

### How It Behaves
Beta can be time-varying and regime-dependent.

### Key Data to Watch
- Historical beta
- Rolling beta
- Adjusted beta

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Institutions target specific portfolio betas and use overlays to adjust exposure.

### Professional Use Cases
- Beta hedging
- Factor tilting

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Portfolio Agent**: Maintains target beta exposure.

### Key Takeaways
Beta quantifies systematic market risk exposure.
