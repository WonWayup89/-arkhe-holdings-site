# Black Scholes

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
The Black-Scholes model prices European options assuming constant volatility.

### Origin
Published in 1973 by Black, Scholes, and Merton.

### Why It Matters
Black-Scholes provided the first closed-form option pricing solution.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
The model solves a partial differential equation under no-arbitrage assumptions.

### How It Behaves
The model assumes constant volatility, leading to systematic mispricing of tails.

### Key Data to Watch
- Implied volatility surface
- Model versus market prices

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Traders use Black-Scholes as a quoting framework while applying stochastic extensions.

### Professional Use Cases
- Option market making
- Volatility surface construction

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Technical Agent**: Uses Black-Scholes Greeks for exposure management.

### Key Takeaways
Black-Scholes remains the foundational option pricing model.
