# Factor Models

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Factor models explain asset returns through exposure to systematic risk factors.

### Origin
CAPM (single-factor) was introduced in the 1960s; multi-factor models followed in the 1990s.

### Why It Matters
Factor models provide a structured framework for risk and return decomposition.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Common factors include market, size, value, momentum, and quality.

### How It Behaves
Factors exhibit time-varying premia and can experience prolonged drawdowns.

### Key Data to Watch
- Factor loadings
- Factor correlation matrix

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Quant funds run pure factor portfolios or use factors as building blocks.

### Professional Use Cases
- Factor-based portfolio construction
- Risk attribution

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **ML Agent**: Dynamically weights factors.

### Key Takeaways
Factor models are the quantitative language of systematic risk.
