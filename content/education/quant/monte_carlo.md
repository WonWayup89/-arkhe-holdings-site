# Monte Carlo Simulation

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Monte Carlo simulation uses repeated random sampling to model probability distributions of outcomes.

### Origin
Developed during the Manhattan Project in the 1940s.

### Why It Matters
Monte Carlo is the standard tool for valuing complex derivatives and stress-testing portfolios.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Simulations generate thousands of possible price paths from assumed distributions.

### How It Behaves
Accuracy improves with more simulations but is limited by input quality.

### Key Data to Watch
- Number of simulation paths
- Convergence of output statistics

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Institutions use Monte Carlo for option pricing and portfolio stress testing.

### Professional Use Cases
- Derivative valuation
- Portfolio-level risk distributions

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Risk Agent**: Runs real-time Monte Carlo for tail-risk.

### Key Takeaways
Monte Carlo quantifies uncertainty in complex financial systems.
