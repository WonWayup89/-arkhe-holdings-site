# Probability

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Probability is the mathematical study of chance and uncertainty in financial outcomes.

### Origin
Formalized in the 17th century and applied to finance after the 1950s.

### Why It Matters
All trading and investment decisions are fundamentally probabilistic.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Probability distributions describe possible returns, while expected value and variance quantify risk and reward.

### How It Behaves
Financial probabilities are often non-normal with fat tails.

### Key Data to Watch
- Expected value
- Variance and standard deviation
- Skew and kurtosis

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Institutions use probability in risk models, option pricing, and portfolio optimization.

### Professional Use Cases
- Probabilistic forecasting
- Risk budgeting
- Bayesian updating

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **ML Agent**: Maintains probabilistic models across agents.

### Key Takeaways
Probability is the language of uncertainty in financial decision-making.
