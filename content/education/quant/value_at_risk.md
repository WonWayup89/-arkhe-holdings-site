# Value at Risk

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Value at Risk (VaR) estimates the maximum potential loss of a portfolio over a given time horizon at a specified confidence level.

### Origin
VaR became standard after RiskMetrics in 1994.

### Why It Matters
VaR provides a single-number risk summary comparable across assets.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
VaR can be parametric, historical, or Monte Carlo based.

### How It Behaves
VaR underestimates tail risk during regime shifts.

### Key Data to Watch
- 99% VaR
- Backtesting exceptions

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Institutions supplement VaR with expected shortfall and stress testing.

### Professional Use Cases
- Daily risk limit monitoring
- Capital allocation

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Risk Agent**: Computes multi-method VaR.

### Key Takeaways
VaR is a useful but incomplete risk metric.
