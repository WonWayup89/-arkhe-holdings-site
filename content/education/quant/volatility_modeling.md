# Volatility Modeling

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Volatility modeling forecasts the magnitude of price fluctuations over time.

### Origin
GARCH models were introduced in the 1980s.

### Why It Matters
Accurate volatility forecasts are essential for risk management and option pricing.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Models capture volatility clustering and mean reversion.

### How It Behaves
Volatility is persistent and exhibits fat tails.

### Key Data to Watch
- Realized versus implied volatility
- Volatility term structure

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Risk departments use volatility models for daily VaR and option pricing.

### Professional Use Cases
- Dynamic risk limits
- Volatility surface construction

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Risk Agent**: Incorporates volatility forecasts into portfolio risk.

### Key Takeaways
Volatility modeling is central to quantitative risk management.
