# Counterparty Risk

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Counterparty risk is the risk that the other party in a transaction defaults.

### Origin
Counterparty risk gained prominence after Lehman’s 2008 failure.

### Why It Matters
Counterparty failure can cascade through the financial system.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Counterparty risk is mitigated by collateral, netting, and central clearing.

### How It Behaves
Risk spikes during market stress when counterparties are most likely to default.

### Key Data to Watch
- Credit default swap spreads on counterparties
- Collateral coverage ratios

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Institutions diversify counterparties and use central clearing where possible.

### Professional Use Cases
- Prime broker diversification
- Collateral optimization

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Risk Agent**: Monitors counterparty exposure in real time.

### Key Takeaways
Counterparty risk is a systemic amplifier during crises.
