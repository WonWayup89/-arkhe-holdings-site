# Liquidity Risk

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Liquidity risk is the risk that an asset cannot be sold quickly at a fair price.

### Origin
Liquidity risk became central after 2008 and 2020 crises.

### Why It Matters
Liquidity risk can turn solvent institutions illiquid.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Liquidity risk includes market liquidity and funding liquidity.

### How It Behaves
Liquidity evaporates during stress, creating feedback loops.

### Key Data to Watch
- Bid-ask spreads
- Market depth

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Institutions maintain liquidity buffers and stress-test funding needs.

### Professional Use Cases
- Liquidity coverage ratio management

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Liquidity Agent**: Monitors real-time liquidity stress.

### Key Takeaways
Liquidity risk is the most dangerous risk because it appears suddenly.
