**Risk Management**

### Beginner Level

**What Is Risk Management?**  
Risk management is the process of identifying, measuring, and controlling possible losses in a trade, portfolio, or business system.

**Origin**  
Modern risk management expanded after major financial shocks including the 1987 crash, the LTCM collapse, the dot-com crash, and the 2008 financial crisis.

**Why It Matters**  
Risk management determines survival. A trader or system can be right often and still fail if losses are not controlled.

### Intermediate Level

**Market Mechanics**  
Core tools include stop-losses, position sizing, Value at Risk, drawdown limits, diversification, volatility targeting, hedging, and stress testing.

**How Risk Management Behaves**  
Good risk systems reduce catastrophic losses and preserve capital through bad regimes. Poor systems often fail during volatility spikes and liquidity shortages.

**Key Data to Watch**  
Maximum drawdown, volatility, Sharpe ratio, Sortino ratio, risk of ruin, leverage, margin, exposure, and correlation.

### Advanced Level

**Institutional Behavior**  
Institutions run risk limits, stress tests, Monte Carlo models, margin controls, kill switches, and scenario analysis across portfolios.

**Professional Use Cases**  
Volatility-adjusted sizing, circuit breakers, portfolio insurance, tail hedging, and liquidity risk controls.

**AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe**  
Risk Agent forecasts drawdowns and adjusts sizing. Supervisor Agent enforces kill switches. Swarm Agent can override bullish signals when risk conditions deteriorate.

**Key Takeaways**  
Risk management is the only edge that cannot be arbitraged away.
