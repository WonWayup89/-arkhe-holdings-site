# Systemic Risk

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Systemic risk is the risk that the failure of one institution or market segment threatens the entire financial system.

### Origin
Systemic risk became a regulatory focus after 2008.

### Why It Matters
Systemic risk can cause economy-wide damage far beyond individual losses.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Systemic risk arises from interconnectedness, leverage, and common exposures.

### How It Behaves
Systemic events are rare but have outsized impact.

### Key Data to Watch
- Interconnectedness metrics
- Common exposure concentrations

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Regulators and institutions run system-wide stress tests.

### Professional Use Cases
- Macroprudential policy design
- Systemic risk buffers

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Supervisor Agent**: Aggregates swarm signals for systemic risk detection.

### Key Takeaways
Systemic risk is the ultimate tail risk that requires macroprudential oversight.
