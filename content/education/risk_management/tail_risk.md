# Tail Risk

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Tail risk is the risk of extreme, low-probability events in the tails of the return distribution.

### Origin
Tail risk became central after 1987 and 2008 crises.

### Why It Matters
Tail events can dominate long-term returns.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Tail risk is measured by expected shortfall and scenario analysis.

### How It Behaves
Tail risk is often underestimated because historical data contains few extremes.

### Key Data to Watch
- Expected shortfall at 99.9%
- Portfolio kurtosis

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Institutions maintain explicit tail-risk hedging programs.

### Professional Use Cases
- Tail-risk hedging overlays

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Risk Agent**: Simulates synthetic tail events.

### Key Takeaways
Tail risk management is essential for institutional survival.
