# Macro Trading

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Macro trading takes positions based on top-down economic and geopolitical views.

### Origin
Gained prominence in the 1970s–1980s.

### Why It Matters
Macro trading profits from major policy and geopolitical shifts.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Traders use currencies, rates, and commodities to express views.

### How It Behaves
Performance depends on forecast accuracy and risk management.

### Key Data to Watch
- Central bank policy paths
- Growth and inflation differentials

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Global macro funds run concentrated, high-conviction portfolios.

### Professional Use Cases
- Directional bets on currencies
- Thematic trades around policy shifts

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Macro Agent**: Synthesizes data into regime probabilities.

### Key Takeaways
Macro trading is the highest-conviction expression of top-down analysis.
