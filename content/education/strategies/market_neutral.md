# Market Neutral

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Market-neutral strategies generate returns with minimal correlation to overall market direction.

### Origin
Formalized by A.W. Jones in 1949.

### Why It Matters
Market-neutral approaches provide beta-independent alpha.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Strategies maintain balanced long and short exposures.

### How It Behaves
Performance is driven by manager skill rather than market direction.

### Key Data to Watch
- Net and gross exposure
- Sharpe ratio relative to beta

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Institutions allocate for consistent, low-volatility returns.

### Professional Use Cases
- Equity market-neutral portfolios
- Fixed-income relative value

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **Portfolio Agent**: Optimizes market-neutral sleeve.

### Key Takeaways
Market-neutral strategies isolate idiosyncratic opportunities.
