# Statistical Arbitrage

## Beginner Level

### What Is It?
Statistical arbitrage exploits temporary pricing inefficiencies identified through statistical models.

### Origin
Developed by quant teams in the 1980s.

### Why It Matters
Statistical arbitrage is a scalable source of alpha in liquid markets.

## Intermediate Level

### Market Mechanics
Models scan for mean-reverting relationships and execute diversified trades.

### How It Behaves
Edge decays over time as competition increases.

### Key Data to Watch
- Signal decay rates
- Capacity constraints

## Advanced Level

### Institutional Behavior
Quant funds run high-capacity stat-arb books.

### Professional Use Cases
- Equity stat-arb portfolios
- Cross-asset statistical relationships

### AI Interpretation in Systems Like Arkhe
- **ML Agent**: Discovers and retires statistical signals.

### Key Takeaways
Statistical arbitrage harvests small, repeatable inefficiencies.
